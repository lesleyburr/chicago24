Backyard Historian

Are you a history detective? Do you notice things around you that others miss? Are you constantly searching for more answers? Then you are a Backyard Historian. Let CHM’s backyard become your playground with this package of insightful tours.

History PUP Crawl

6:00–7:30 p.m.

Take your four-legged friend on epic walk! Find out the tales of historic pups on this two-mile tour that immerses you and your best friend in beautiful Lincoln Park. Visit its numerous gardens, find out about pup-friendly pubs, and more on this hound-dog history tour. All humans must have a ticket; K9s must bring their humans and vice versa.

Location:

Meet at CHM plaza.

Garden of the Dead: The History of Lincoln Park’s Elusive Graves

10:00–12:00 a.m.

Chicago’s early dead were trafficked into and out of its first municipal graveyard, the Chicago City Cemetery. Pamela Bannos of the Hidden Truths Project shares stories of grave robbers, improper burials, and the confusion that led to the creation of Lincoln Park.

Location:

Meet at the Museum.

Old Town

10:00 a.m.–12:00 p.m.

From Germans to hippies to yuppies, Old Town has seen an assortment of residents come and go. Spend a morning learning about this neighborhood’s colorful past and vibrant present.

Location:

Meet at CHM unless otherwise noted. Tour running time: 90 minutes to two hours.

Chicago by Day and Night

5:00–9:00 p.m.

Travel back in time with Chicagoans Paul Durica and Bill Savage, who, if born earlier, might have written chapters for Chicago By Day and Night, a guidebook published for visitors to Chicago during the 1893 world's fair. Follow the footsteps of the adventurous visitor as your guides provide colorful context and an informative introduction to a wildly entertaining journey through the Chicago of 120 years ago.

Location:

Tour meets at the Museum.

Graceland Cemetery Tour

10:00 a.m.–12:00 p.m.

Experience the history of old Chicago as you stroll through one of the country’s most famous cemeteries. Join historian Al Walavich on a tour of the graves of Graceland, including Marshall Field, Louis Sullivan, Potter and Bertha Palmer, and George Pullman. Meet at the cemetery entrance at Irving Park Road and Clark Street.

Location:

All tours begin at cemetery entrance: Irving Park Road and North Clark Street.

2:00–4:00 p.m.

Location:
